Welcome to Houston

Overview

Houston is the largest city in Texas and the fourth-largest city in the United States. Known as "Space City" for being home to NASA's Johnson Space Center, Houston is a major hub for the energy industry, particularly oil and natural gas. The city boasts a diverse population, world-class museums, and a thriving culinary scene.

History

Founded in 1836 by brothers Augustus and John Allen, Houston was named after General Sam Houston, who won Texas's independence from Mexico. The city grew rapidly after the discovery of oil at Spindletop in 1901. Houston became a major port city with the opening of the Houston Ship Channel in 1914, and later emerged as a center for the space industry.

Culture

Houston's culture reflects its diverse population, with strong influences from Mexican, Vietnamese, and African American communities. The city is known for its performing arts, including the Houston Grand Opera and Houston Ballet. Houston's food scene is renowned, particularly for its barbecue, Tex-Mex, and Vietnamese cuisine. The city also hosts the annual Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, the world's largest of its kind.
