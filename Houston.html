Welcome to Houston

Overview

Houston is the largest city in Texas and the fourth-largest city in the United States. Known as "Space City," it's home to NASA's Johnson Space Center. Houston is a major hub for the energy industry, particularly oil and natural gas, and has a diverse economy with strong healthcare, manufacturing, and aerospace sectors.

History

Founded in 1836 by brothers Augustus and John Allen, Houston was named after General Sam Houston, who won Texas's independence from Mexico. The city grew rapidly after the discovery of oil at Spindletop in 1901 and became a major center for the oil industry. The establishment of NASA's Manned Spacecraft Center (now Johnson Space Center) in 1961 further cemented Houston's importance.

Culture

Houston is one of the most diverse cities in the United States, with a rich cultural scene that includes the Houston Museum District, Theater District, and numerous festivals. The city is known for its world-class museums, performing arts, and diverse culinary scene. Houston's sports culture is also significant, with professional teams in all major sports.
