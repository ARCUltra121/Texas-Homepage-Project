Welcome to Texas

Overview

Texas is the second-largest state in the United States by both area and population, known as the "Lone Star State". It's a diverse state with a rich history, ranging from its independent Republic of Texas era to its current status as a major player in the US economy. Texas is renowned for its cowboy culture, large cities, diverse landscapes, and vibrant Tex-Mex cuisine.

History

Texas's story begins with long-established Indigenous cultures. Spanish explorers arrived, and it became part of New Spain, then Mexico. American settlers grew in number, sparking the Texas Revolution (Alamo, Goliad) and the Republic of Texas (1836-1845). U.S. annexation in 1845 led to the Mexican-American War. Texas then joined the Confederacy during the Civil War before rejoining the Union. The late 19th and 20th centuries saw booms in cattle and oil, shaping its modern economy and distinctive identity.

Culture

Texas culture is a vibrant blend of influences. Its history fosters a strong sense of independence and pride. The "cowboy" image, though a part of the past, still resonates, symbolizing self-reliance. A love for Tex-Mex cuisine, country music, and Friday night football are common threads. You'll find a generally friendly and outgoing spirit, often expressed with a distinctive drawl. The vastness of the land contributes to a sense of scale and a diverse landscape that mirrors its people. While increasingly urban and diverse, a core Texan identity remains strong.

Economy

Economy: Texas has a massive and diverse economy, the second-largest in the United States. Historically known for agriculture and oil, its modern economy is driven by a wide range of sectors, including technology, aerospace, energy, healthcare, and agriculture. Geography

Geography: Texas is known for its vast and diverse landscape, including the Texas Hill Country, the Gulf Coast, and the Big Bend region. It's also home to the second-largest city in the country, Houston, and the largest city in the world, Dallas-Fort Worth.
