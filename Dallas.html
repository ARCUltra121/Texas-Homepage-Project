Welcome to Dallas

Overview

Dallas is the third-largest city in Texas and the ninth-largest in the United States. Known as "The Big D," Dallas is a major business and cultural hub, home to numerous Fortune 500 companies. The city is famous for its arts district, professional sports teams, and as the site of the assassination of President John F. Kennedy.

History

Founded in 1841 by John Neely Bryan, Dallas grew from a small trading post to a major city after the arrival of railroads in the 1870s. The city became a center for banking and insurance in the early 20th century. Dallas gained international attention in 1963 with the assassination of President Kennedy, and later became known for its role in the television series "Dallas" and its portrayal of the oil industry.

Culture

Dallas is known for its vibrant arts scene, including the Dallas Arts District, the largest urban arts district in the nation. The city hosts numerous cultural events and festivals throughout the year. Dallas is also famous for its professional sports teams, including the Dallas Cowboys (NFL) and Dallas Mavericks (NBA). The city's food scene features famous barbecue and Tex-Mex cuisine.
