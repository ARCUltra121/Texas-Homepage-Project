Welcome to Dallas

Overview

Dallas is the third-largest city in Texas and the ninth-largest city in the United States. Known as "The Big D," Dallas is a major business and cultural center in North Texas. The city is famous for its modern architecture, vibrant arts district, and as the home of the Dallas Cowboys football team.

History

Founded in 1841 by John Neely Bryan, Dallas grew from a small trading post to a major city. The city's growth was spurred by the construction of railroads in the late 19th century and the discovery of oil in East Texas in the 1930s. Dallas became a major center for banking and commerce, and today it's known for its strong economy and business-friendly environment.

Culture

Dallas is known for its vibrant arts scene, including the Dallas Arts District, which is the largest contiguous urban arts district in the nation. The city is home to numerous museums, theaters, and music venues. Dallas is also famous for its sports culture, particularly football, and its distinctive Texas cuisine, including barbecue and Tex-Mex.
